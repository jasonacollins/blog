Darwin and Marx

5 July 2011
Yesterday I visited Down House, Charles Darwin’s home from 1842 until his death in 1882. Darwin wrote most of his major works there. The house contained a lot of interesting artefacts and bits of information, but one of the more interesting was a copy of Das Kapitalsent from Karl Marx to Darwin. Inscribed inside the book was: Mr. Charles Darwin On the part of his sincere admirer Karl Marx London 16 June 1873 Modena Villas Maitland Park Seeing this, I did a quick search to find out the extent to which Marx was in contact with and influenced by Darwin (having not yet read Das Kapital) and it seems that there is a history of storytelling and exaggeration around it.
